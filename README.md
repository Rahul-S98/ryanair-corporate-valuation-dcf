# ryanair-corporate-valuation-dcf
An institutional-grade multi-period Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF) valuation model for Ryanair Holdings plc built using audited IFRS financial data.
An institutional-grade multi-period Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF) valuation model for Ryanair Holdings plc built using audited IFRS financial data. The Investment Narrative & Core Thesis In the current volatile European aviation landscape - characterized by fluctuating jet fuel prices, geopolitical airspace restrictions, and macro consumer cost pressures - traditional airline equity is heavily discounted due to high beta exposure. This project develops a fundamental evaluation framework to answer a critical investment question: Does the market accurately price the operational moat of Europe’s lowest-cost carrier, or does a deep-value mismatch exist? By building an uncompromised, automated Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) valuation architecture, this project evaluates the intrinsic enterprise worth of Ryanair Holdings plc (RYAAY). Rather than relying on third-party aggregated tools, this engine integrates raw data extracted from audited International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) annual press releases and processes it through a multi-period forecasting matrix. The core thesis reveals that Ryanair operates less like a cyclical airline and more like a high-efficiency manufacturing machine, protected by an ironclad balance sheet, unparalleled cost containment, and localized corporate tax structural advantages. Step-by-Step Corporate Finance Architecture 1. The Historical Baseline & Margin Audit Every reliable financial projection begins by understanding structural baseline constraints. I extracted and reconstructed a 3-year historical matrix directly from the Group Consolidated Financial Statements: • Top-Line Velocity: Operating revenue expanded from €13,443.80M in FY24 to €15,544.30M in FY26, proving resilient consumer traffic demand despite broader macroeconomic stagnation. • Operating Efficiency Tracking: Operating profit (EBIT) showed an inflection recovery to €2,374.20M in FY26, capturing an underlying 15.3% trailing EBIT margin. This metric serves as the anchor point for normalized structural performance. • Capital Reinvestment Intrinsic Costs: Capital expenditure profiles tracked through a cyclical peak of €2,391.90M in FY24 down to €1,892.40M in FY26 as aircraft expansion deliveries balanced against steady depreciation baselines (€1,373.40M in FY26).
